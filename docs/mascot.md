# kodex Mascot — Image Generation Prompt

## Concept

A raven inspired by Norse mythology. In Norse legend, Odin had two ravens — Huginn ("thought") and Muninn ("memory") — who flew across the entire world each day and returned to whisper everything they had seen. kodex does the same for codebases: it flies across every file, every symbol, every connection the compiler resolved, and reports back with perfect recall.

## Visual Prompt

A single raven, sharp and intelligent, perched on an open ancient codex (book). The raven is mid-turn, head tilted slightly as if it has just spotted something — alert, precise, knowing. One eye is visible and glows faintly with a cool violet or electric blue light, suggesting machine-level perception. The codex beneath it has pages filled with faint, abstract symbols resembling code — curly braces, arrows, type signatures — not literal code, but suggestive of structured knowledge.

Thin, luminous threads of light (violet/blue) extend outward from the raven like a subtle web or constellation, representing the call graphs and symbol connections kodex maps. The threads connect to small floating glyphs — a function signature here, a type reference there — dissolving into the dark background.

## Style

- **Aesthetic:** Dark, minimal, refined. Not cartoonish — closer to a stylized emblem or sigil.
- **Palette:** Black, charcoal, deep matte navy. Accents in electric violet (#7C3AED) or cool blue (#3B82F6). Minimal white for contrast on the codex pages.
- **Rendering:** Flat or semi-flat with subtle texture. Think risograph print or high-end developer tool branding. No gradients, no 3D, no photorealism.
- **Composition:** Centered, square aspect ratio (1:1). The raven and codex form the focal point. The luminous threads fill the negative space without clutter.
- **Line work:** Clean, confident strokes. Feathers are geometric — angular, faceted, not fluffy. The raven should feel sharp and architectural.

## Variations to Generate

1. **Icon / Favicon** — Raven head only, profile view, minimal detail. Works at 32x32px. Glowing eye is the key feature.
2. **Full emblem** — Raven perched on codex with luminous threads. Square format for README, docs, splash screen.
3. **CLI banner** — Horizontal composition. Raven on the left, "kodex" wordmark on the right in a monospace or geometric sans-serif font. Subtle threads connecting them.
4. **Dark mode badge** — Circular badge format. Raven silhouette inside a circle, threads radiating to the circle's edge like a compass or circuit diagram.

## What to Avoid

- Cartoonish or cute proportions — the raven should feel sharp, not friendly
- Overly detailed feathers or photorealistic rendering
- Bright or warm colors — no reds, oranges, yellows
- Generic "bird logo" shapes — it must read as a raven specifically (thick beak, shaggy throat feathers, heavy build)
- Crowded composition — let the dark space breathe

## Mythology Reference (for context, not literal depiction)

> "Every morning Odin sends them out over the whole world, and they come back at breakfast time. In this way he gets information about many things." — Prose Edda

The raven is not decorative. It is an instrument of total knowledge.
